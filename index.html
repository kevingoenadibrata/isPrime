What is a Prime Number?

A prime number is a positive integer that is divisible only by 1 and the number itself. Positive integers that has an integer divisor other than 1 and itself is called, a composite number.

For example:
    >7 has only two integer divisors: 1 and 7. Therefore, 7 is a prime number
    >9 can be divided by 3, which is not divisible with just 1 or the number itself. So, 9 is not a prime number. It is a composite number

Special Number: 1

Number 1 is a positive integer but it only has one integer divisor: 1. A prime number must have exactly two integer divisors. Therefore 1 is not a prime number.

Special Number: 2

Number 2 is the only even prime number. It is divisible only by 1 and itself (2).
